Industry Changes - Ofcom 08 numbers

Plans are still going ahead for Summer 2015 to make it simpler for individuals to understand the costs of calling an 08 number. Under new rules set by Ofcom, callers contacting service numbers will see the costs split in two, one being an 'access charge' going to their provider, the other a 'service charge' to the company they are calling. On top of this 0800 / 0808 numbers will be free for consumers to call from mobile phones, as they are currently for landlines.

Increased uptake of Virtual Contact Centre

Virtual Contact Centres are a Cloud based solution which allows customers to contact services through their preferred method of communication. This could be via web chat, email, social networking sites such as Linkedin and Twitter, or even the generic phone call.

Where traditional call centres work generally from one location and are contactable only through call or email facilities, Virtual Contact Centres have the ability to work from multiple locations i.e, the office, an agents home or on the go with the ability to overview every interaction from the organisations main office. A 360 degrees visual on the centre, through live wall boards allows supervisors to view, monitor and record which calls have been answered, lost or are queuing.

Companies in the past would have invested heavy money into physical hardware to increase office productivity, as well as the original set up capital. Through a VCC, office productivity is created through a scalable solution meaning as the business or organisation grows the software can be expanded to accommodate. Other features include focus on calls being answered through skills based routing which automatically distribute calls to agents depending on skills set, with open source API integrations with CRM’s such as Salesforce and Zensdesk and a granular reporting facility when agents are on breaks.

Virtual Contact Centres are easy to deploy, figure and use, therefore would be an asset to update your traditional centre.

IP Solutions set up Virtual Contact Centre in Bulgaria

Reward Gateway is a global company focused on bringing a company’s employees together through online platforms. Originally based in Notting Hill, Reward Gateway wanted to move their contact centres to Bulgaria but still be able to control and communicate from their main offices in London.

With no phone system and only an internet connection in place, Reward Gateway had to work quickly to meet the deadline of their new office opening. Within two weeks of the customer moving forward with us through a cloud based hosted, telephony solution, they went live.

During the training process at the Bulgarian office, we created a ratio split on inbound calls, 70% of calls continued going to the UK office while the new call centre found its feet, as they progressed the ratio spun around until the London call centre was closed.

The Bulgarian location is open 24/7 with 35 members of staff on the phones at all times, able to accommodate clients from all over the world. We created local cloud based Australian, US and UK numbers for each country, to contact the Bulgarian call centre, at their local charge rate. Agents have full visibility to see where each call is coming from and answer accordingly, customers are also able to contact the service through webchat and email ensuring Reward Gateway provides the best customer engagement.

Reward Gateway was able to deploy a global telephony and VCC solutions where calls are delivered to the call centre from any destination, yet still be managed by the London office.
